HOW TO USE THE EARLY RUNNER DETECTOR
=====================================

WHAT IT DOES
------------
The detector watches a stream of 1-minute price/volume bars for a single
Solana token. It maintains a rolling 30-minute window of "quiet" baseline
data, then watches for a breakout — a sudden jump in both volume and price.

Once a breakout is detected, it waits 15 minutes, computes five features
about the breakout's character, and issues a WINNER or LOSER prediction.

- WINNER means: the breakout pattern looks like tokens that went on to
  gain 5x-800x from their quiet period price.
- LOSER means: the pattern looks like tokens that spiked 1.5x-6x and
  then crashed 70-100% back down.


HOW TO RUN IT
-------------
1. Import the detector:
     from detector import RunnerDetector
     det = RunnerDetector(token_address="<token_mint_address>")

2. Feed 1-minute OHLCV bars as they arrive:
     alert = det.feed_bar({
         "unix_time": 1234567890,
         "o": 0.001,      # open price
         "h": 0.0012,     # high price
         "l": 0.0009,     # low price
         "c": 0.0011,     # close price
         "v": 50000       # volume in USD
     })

3. If alert is not None, a prediction has been made:
     alert["prediction"]        # "winner" or "loser"
     alert["total_score"]       # numeric score (-5 to +5)
     alert["current_multiplier"] # how far price moved from baseline
     alert["feature_details"]   # breakdown of each rule

4. To plug into a Birdeye WebSocket:
   - Subscribe to 1-minute candle updates for a token
   - Convert each candle to the bar format above
   - Feed into the detector
   - Handle alerts as desired


WHAT EACH ALERT FIELD MEANS
----------------------------
token_address:       The Solana mint address being monitored
breakout_time:       Unix timestamp when the breakout was first detected
current_price:       Price at the time of scoring (15 min after breakout)
baseline_price:      Median price during the 30-minute quiet period before breakout
current_multiplier:  current_price / baseline_price (e.g. 2.5x means 150% gain)
total_score:         Sum of all rule scores (-5 to +5). Higher = more winner-like.
prediction:          "winner" if score >= 0, "loser" if score < 0
feature_details:     List of each rule with raw value, threshold, and +1/-1 score


THE FIVE SCORING RULES
----------------------
Each rule adds +1 (winner-like) or -1 (loser-like) to the total score.

1. PRICE MOVE FROM BASELINE (strongest signal, Cohen's d = 0.94)
   Measures how far price has moved from the quiet period average.
   Winners move further from baseline in the first 15 minutes.
   Threshold: ln(1 + value) > 0.865 → score +1
   In plain English: price above ~1.4x the baseline = good sign

2. VOLUME RATIO (Cohen's d = 0.60)
   Average breakout volume divided by baseline median volume.
   Paradoxically, LOWER ratios favor winners. Pump-and-dumps blast volume
   artificially; organic breakouts have more modest but sustained volume.
   Threshold: ln(1 + value) < 1.601 → score +1
   In plain English: volume under ~4x baseline = more likely organic

3. VOLUME CONSISTENCY (Cohen's d = 0.51)
   What fraction of the 15 breakout bars had volume above baseline median.
   Lower consistency favors winners — organic buying is lumpy (some bars hot,
   some cold), while pump bots maintain constant high volume.
   Threshold: ln(1 + value) < 0.470 → score +1

4. PRICE RETRACEMENT DEPTH (Cohen's d = 0.37)
   The deepest pullback during the 15 minutes, as a fraction of the total move.
   Shallower pullbacks favor winners — strong demand catches dips quickly.
   Threshold: ln(1 + value) < 0.406 → score +1
   In plain English: pullbacks under ~50% of the move = strong demand

5. VOLUME STREAK (Cohen's d = 0.32)
   Longest consecutive run of bars with volume above 2x baseline.
   Shorter streaks favor winners — organic accumulation comes in waves,
   not one sustained blast from a single bot/whale.
   Threshold: ln(1 + value) < 1.386 → score +1


SCORE THRESHOLD
---------------
Threshold: 0 (predict WINNER if score >= 0, LOSER if score < 0)

This threshold was chosen to maximize the true positive rate (catching
real winners) while keeping the false positive rate below 40%.


EXPECTED ACCURACY
-----------------
Based on leave-one-out cross-validation on 81 historical tokens:

  Accuracy:  59% (correctly classifies about 3 in 5 tokens)
  Precision: 47% (when it says "winner", it's right about half the time)
  Recall:    55% (it catches about half of actual winners)

This is better than random (50%) but not a reliable standalone signal.
It should be one input among several in a trading decision.


WHAT THE DETECTOR CANNOT TELL YOU
---------------------------------
This detector uses ONLY price and volume data from the first 15 minutes.
It has NO information about:

1. ON-CHAIN RISK FACTORS
   - Whether the liquidity pool is locked or can be pulled (rug risk)
   - How concentrated token ownership is (whale dump risk)
   - Whether the contract has mint authority still enabled
   - Whether there are suspicious wallet patterns (wash trading)

2. SOCIAL/SENTIMENT SIGNALS
   - Twitter/Telegram mention velocity and sentiment
   - Whether the pump is driven by a known influencer or KOL
   - Whether there's an organic community or just bots

3. TOKEN FUNDAMENTALS
   - Age of the token and its trading history beyond 30 minutes
   - Market cap and liquidity depth relative to volume
   - Whether the token has been on exchanges or just on-chain

4. BROADER MARKET CONTEXT
   - Whether SOL or BTC is trending up or down
   - Overall meme coin market sentiment
   - Time of day / day of week effects

IMPORTANT: A "WINNER" signal does NOT mean "buy this token." It means
the early breakout pattern RESEMBLES tokens that historically went on
to large gains. Many factors beyond price/volume determine whether a
token is safe to trade or a potential rug pull.

Always verify on-chain safety (RugCheck, Birdeye security score) before
acting on any signal from this detector.
