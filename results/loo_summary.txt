LEAVE-ONE-OUT VALIDATION SUMMARY
============================================================

How this test works:
1. Remove one token from the dataset
2. Rebuild the scoring rules from the remaining tokens
3. Use those rules to predict the removed token
4. Check if the prediction was correct
5. Repeat for every token

This tests whether the rules are robust or just memorizing the training data.
If LOO accuracy is close to the training accuracy, the rules generalize well.
If LOO accuracy is much lower, the rules are overfit.

Results:
  Accuracy:  59.26% (48 correct out of 81)
  Precision: 47.22%
    When the rules predicted 'winner', they were right 47% of the time.
  Recall:    54.84%
    Of all 31 actual winners, the rules caught 17.

Confusion Matrix:
                  Predicted Winner  Predicted Loser
Actual Winner                   17               14
Actual Loser                    19               31

Comparison to training accuracy:
  Training accuracy (Phase 3): 67.90%
  LOO accuracy: 59.26%
  Gap: 8.6% — MODERATE. Some overfitting but rules are still useful.

VERDICT:
This classifier has limited utility. 59% accuracy is below the 65% threshold.

What would likely improve it:
- On-chain data: wallet concentration, unique buyers, LP lock status
- Social signals: Twitter/Telegram mention velocity
- Token metadata: age, holder distribution, developer activity
- More training data: 100+ tokens per category
